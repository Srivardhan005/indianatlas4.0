Rivers of India

Ganges River

The Ganges River, flowing over 2,500 km, is one of India's most revered and longest rivers, vital to the cultural and spiritual lives of millions.

Yamuna River

The Yamuna River, a major tributary of the Ganges, is approximately 1,376 km long and holds great significance in Indian mythology and culture.

Godavari River

The Godavari River, known as the 'Dakshin Ganga' or 'Ganges of the South', stretches over 1,465 km and is a lifeline for the states it flows through.

Narmada River

The Narmada River, flowing westward for about 1,312 km, is considered one of India's holiest rivers and is known for its scenic beauty and spiritual significance.

Krishna River

The Krishna River, stretching over 1,400 km, is a major waterway in southern India, vital for irrigation and known for the Krishna Pushkaram festival.

Indus River

The Indus River, one of the longest in the world, spans over 3,180 km, flowing through India and Pakistan, and is crucial to the region's history and economy.
