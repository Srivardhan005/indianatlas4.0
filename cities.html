Historic Cities

Varanasi

One of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world, Varanasi is a spiritual capital with sacred ghats along the Ganges River. It's a hub for Hinduism, Buddhism, and Jainism. The city is renowned for its vibrant culture, classical music, and educational institutions.

Delhi

With a history spanning over a thousand years, Delhi is a tapestry of empires, including the Mughals and the British. It boasts iconic monuments like the Red Fort and Qutub Minar. The city is a melting pot of diverse cultures, offering a blend of old-world charm and modern sophistication.

Jaipur

Known as the Pink City, Jaipur is renowned for its palaces and forts, such as the Hawa Mahal and Amber Fort. It is a vibrant cultural hub with rich traditions and heritage. The city's architectural splendor and colorful bazaars make it a top tourist destination.

Hyderabad

Founded in the 16th century, Hyderabad is famed for the Charminar and Golconda Fort. It has a unique blend of Hindu and Islamic traditions, reflected in its rich cultural heritage. The city is also known for its pearls, biryani, and high-tech industries.

Kolkata

Once the capital of British India, Kolkata is a city of colonial architecture and cultural vibrancy. The Victoria Memorial and Howrah Bridge are among its iconic landmarks. The city is also known for its literary heritage, art, and vibrant festivals like Durga Puja.

Mumbai

Mumbai, the financial capital of India, is known for its fast-paced lifestyle, Bollywood film industry, and diverse culture. Key attractions include the Gateway of India, Marine Drive, and the Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus. The city is also famous for its street food and bustling markets.
